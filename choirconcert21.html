Trinity's Choir Has Its Spring Choir Concert In The ATO Chapel


Trinity International University's choir sang "Songs and Prayers of Praise and Worship" at their
spring concert on April 29th at 7:30 PM in the ATO chapel.

The concert has been in the making for the entire semester, and some choir members stated they
felt it would never come.

"It was great to see the hard work that multiple of my classmates put in to make this concert a
success pay off," concert attendant Jakob Flores said. "[I] definitely was not expecting what I
saw, with this being my first time at something like this."

The event ran for approximately two and a half hours. Choir member Mark Akert provided
insight into the practices and the amount of work this concert took to be a success.

"It felt like we have been practicing for forever," Akert said, "Having to battle football and choir
all in one semester was extremely hard."

He also said he appreciated the flexibility his coaches and directors allowed him to have with his
schedule.

The turnout for the event was tremendous, with the chapel infused with people ready and eager
to hear some good music.

"The nerves were most definitely there," Akert said. "Like before a football game [with]
everyone there to see you."

The pressure on these students was amazing, but their performance was even more impressive,
causing many in the crowd to rise out of their seats for the performance.

"I just want to thank everyone who attended, and all my choirmates," said star singer Arthur
Williams.

The Trinity community could not be more proud of these students, especially with finals
approaching and many of the participants also involved in other events on campus.
