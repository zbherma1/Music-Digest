First Edition of Coffee House


The first edition of this year's bi-annual Coffee House event was held on Sunday, October 23, in
the lobby of Johnson Hall.

According to those who were there it was cool to see some of the students' hidden talents, and
the night was full of memorable performances, including Lanz Powell's rap which was by far the
headline of the proceedings according to the attendees.

“Lanz's rap was super unique and very awesome to watch. He poured his heart and soul into it
and he definitely did an amazing job,” said TIU freshman Liam Mickle.

Coffee House is an event that allows students to show off some of their talents and attempt to
make people laugh, although sometimes the actual existence of laughter can be hit or miss.

Coffee House is also a time when Johnson Hall RA's create fellowship with the students, which
is a wonderful thing to have in the small, intimate community of TIU.

"It's a great time for us RAs to get to meet more students while also creating a great experience
for those who attend," said Isaac Foote.

Over the years the event has evolved from a smaller event to where we see it now, as a lot of
students performed and even more were in attendance, with roughly 100 people there watching
the acts.

The event was packed with people in a very small venue. They had desserts and drinks in one of
the corners. It was also fairly dark.

Overall it seems like the students liked the way it was set up and are looking forward to next
semester's Coffee House.

"I liked the desserts being there along with drinks, plus really nice seating from where I was. The
stage set up was very cool. I would go again next year," said Mickle.

It will be interesting to see if any changes are made for the spring semester's event, but the night
was certainly a success. Keep an eye out for much more laughter, fun, and community when the
second one rolls around.
